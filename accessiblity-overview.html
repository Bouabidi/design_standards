Accessibility Overview

Web accessibility, simply put, means that people with disabilities can perceive, understand, naviagte, interact with and contribute as anyone else while on the web. We make our web product conform to these guidelines by developing it in accordance with the guidelines defined by the Web Accessibility Initiative (WIA) in their Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) .

WCAG 2.0 Basics

The guidlines are organized into 4 groups or goals. The below graphic is part of the WCAG 2 at a glance site .


These guidelines are cover in more detail on the developer guidelines page.




There are 3 levels of conformance - A, AA, AAA. Currently our product is A rated with elements rated to AA and AAA. We need to maintain the minimum threshold of A and strive for higher conformity moving forward.




At a minimum, a conformance level of A is required. As we develope our product we should take every opportunity to conform to AA and AAA.

Take the time to review Marth Jack and Mark Eichler's presentation and related documentation for a more comprehensive overview.
